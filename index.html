Turning a support call into a product fix

Call analysis

What is new?

Reviewed an enablement call between our team and a customer's admin, and traced a "content isn't showing up" complaint back to a missing app-analysis step rather than user error.

Why does it matter?

If auto-tags depend on a step the product never surfaces, every customer with the same gap eventually files the same ticket. I proposed auto-suggested keywords at content-creation time — tying it back to accessibility feedback from an NPS responder — so accuracy improves before the ticket ever gets filed.

500+ tickets → a knowledge-gap roadmap

DAP solutioning strategy

What is new?

Structured recurring L1 support patterns into named themes — Self-Help tags & discoverability, feature customization know-hows, content/widget creation bottlenecks — each mapped through current customer journey → why it's a knowledge gap → proposed DAP solutioning.

Why does it matter?

A single help article about a character limit only patches one ticket. Spotting the same issue across dozens of tickets is what gets a product team to fix the actual cause — things like showing the limit upfront, adding a discoverable "always show" setting, or making visibility rules clearer.

Accessibility writing for Whatfix community

Accessibility

What is new?

Tested Beacons and Pop-ups with a JAWS screen reader and wrote general guidelines for creating accessible DAP content, alongside topic-based articles and FAQ/troubleshooting guides.

Why does it matter?

Accessibility guidelines only help if writers know exactly what "accessible" means for the specific widget they're documenting — this gave them that clarity.
